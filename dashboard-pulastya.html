Signal Modes Explained
Conservative
BB touch hard required. Score 7/10 min. Session gate blocks outside 07:00–17:00 UTC. Post-spike cooldown active. Fewest signals, highest quality. Best during volatile news weeks.
Balanced (Default)
BB touch strongly weighted but near-band entries trigger too. Score 5/10 min. Session gate is a warning only. Trend-following SELL signals included. Catches most real setups including the downtrends you were seeing.
Aggressive
No session gate. No spike cooldown. Score 3/10 min. Fires on EMA crosses and trends without BB touch. You decide whether to act. More signals. Not recommended below $50 balance.

Crypto — BTC & ETH
Different engine entirely. Crypto uses wider Bollinger Bands (2.5σ vs 2.0σ for forex), percentage-based distance thresholds instead of pips, and a 5× spike filter instead of 3.5×.

24/7 market — no session gate. But liquidity is thinner on weekends and signals are less reliable from Friday 21:00 UTC to Sunday 21:00 UTC.

Always HIGH RISK — BTC can move $2,000 in one hour. ETH $150. Even a "strong" signal can reverse before your TP is reached.

Position sizing: Use 0.01 lots maximum for BTC. The margin at 1:200 leverage is tiny but the price movement is enormous.

Rule: Do not trade crypto until you have been consistently profitable on EUR/USD for at least 2 weeks. The signals are built in for when you are ready — not for right now.
